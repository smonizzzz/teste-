Sprint Update

V2 Launches Its Infrastructure Layer — Sprint 1 Officially Underway

With roles confirmed and repositories configured, Team V2 has begun laying the architectural groundwork that will connect every team in the project.

"V2 is not just another backend team — we are the contract owners, the integration bridge, and the data layer that makes the whole system possible."

Sprint 1 marks the beginning of a phased delivery plan: this phase focuses on establishing interface contracts and shared conventions before any code runs in production. Team V2 is co-authoring IF1 alongside S2 and V1, and has begun an initial IF2 draft to present to M1. The async standup model — daily Wiki updates replacing synchronous stand-ups — is now active across both timezones.

By Sergio Moniz · Team Lead 26 Mar 2026

IF2 Contract Draft Opens for Review

The Server-to-Monitor interface specification is taking shape. V2 and M1 have begun synchronising on endpoint requirements, payload structure, and authentication flows.

The IF2 contract defines the full REST and WebSocket API surface that both M1 (Patient App) and M2 (Clinical Dashboard) will consume. It must be frozen by the close of Sprint 2. Early alignment now avoids costly rework later.

Six Teams, One Call — First ASM Sets the Tone

All project managers gathered in a synchronous session via WeMeet to align on timelines, communication standards, and cross-team dependencies.

Key outcomes included confirmation of the Git English-only convention, the rotating meeting schedule to share the inconvenient timezone slot fairly, and a mutual agreement to flag blockers proactively rather than waiting for sprint reviews.

Milestone

Team Structure Proposal Published: 28 Students, 6 Teams, One System

A comprehensive team structure document covering all six teams, role definitions, cross-testing models, and coordination mechanisms has been formally published.

The document establishes the foundational principles guiding the entire project: modularity, specialisation, scalability, and parallel development. Each team of five members operates with a PM, a Shadow Vice PM of opposite nationality, a System Architect, and two Programmers who formally cross-review each other's code. V2 is singled out as the Integration Lead from Sprint 3 — a strategic responsibility that places our team at the centre of all end-to-end validation efforts.

✔ Resolved

Sensor Hardware Unavailable in Portugal — Mock Module Unblocks V2

Physical sensors are only available in China, creating a potential development bottleneck for Portuguese team members. S2’s mandatory Sprint 1 deliverable — a software mock module — resolves this entirely.

The mock data module will emit realistic JSON sensor frames at configurable sampling rates, allowing V2 to develop and test the ingestion pipeline without waiting for physical hardware. Hardware-specific tasks remain assigned to Chinese team members only.
