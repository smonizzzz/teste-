01 · Context

Why This Project Exists

Recovering from limb surgery is rarely straightforward. Traditional rehabilitation demands frequent clinic visits and constant supervision — a model that is costly, time-consuming, and inaccessible for many patients. Our project tackles this gap head-on by building an intelligent, sensor-driven assistant that guides patients through their recovery at home.

Developed jointly by UTAD and Jilin University as part of the Distributed Software Development course, the system brings together 28 students across 6 specialised teams, each contributing a distinct technical layer to one unified rehabilitation platform.

02 · What the System Does

Three Core Capabilities

Capability 01

Tell

Delivers instant feedback on whether a patient's movement matches the prescribed rehabilitation motion, reducing the risk of reinjury from poor form.

Capability 02

Reconstruct

Builds a 3D representation of each recorded movement and benchmarks joint angles against clinically expected recovery curves for precise medical review.

Capability 03

Suggest

Analyses a patient's performance history to generate tailored exercise recommendations, adapting the rehabilitation programme as recovery progresses.

03 · How It’s Built

System Architecture

Six teams operate across three independent layers, each exposing a formally specified interface contract. This approach lets every team build and test in isolation while guaranteeing seamless integration when the contracts are met.
